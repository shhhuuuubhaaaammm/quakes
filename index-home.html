Earthquakes are natural phenomena that occur when there is a sudden release of energy in the Earth's crust, creating seismic waves. They can range from minor tremors to major events that cause significant damage and loss of life. The main features of an earthquake include:

  • Epicenter: The point on the Earth's surface directly above the earthquake's origin (focus or hypocenter).
  • Magnitude: The measure of the energy released, commonly quantified by the Richter scale or the moment magnitude scale.
  • Seismic Waves: The waves of energy that travel through the Earth, causing the shaking. There are different types of seismic waves, including primary (P) waves, secondary (S) waves, and surface waves.
  • Aftershocks: Smaller tremors that follow the main shock and can cause additional damage.
