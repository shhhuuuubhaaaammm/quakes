Natural Disasters

  • Ground Shaking: Can cause buildings, bridges, and other structures to collapse.
  • Surface Rupture: Cracks or shifts in the ground that can damage infrastructure.
  • Landslides: Triggered by shaking, leading to additional destruction.
  • Tsunamis: Underwater earthquakes can generate tsunamis, causing widespread flooding.
  • Lava Flows: Can destroy everything in their path.
  • Ash Fall: Can collapse roofs, contaminate water supplies, and cause respiratory problems.
  • Pyroclastic Flows: Fast-moving currents of hot gas and volcanic matter that can incinerate everything in their path.
  • Lahars: Volcanic mudflows that can bury communities.
  • High Winds: Can damage buildings, trees, and power lines.
  • Storm Surge: Coastal flooding caused by the rise in sea level due to the storm.
  • Heavy Rainfall: Can lead to flash floods and landslides.
  • Tornadoes: Often associated with the storm and can cause localized damage.
  • River Flooding: Can submerge homes, infrastructure, and agricultural land.
  • Flash Flooding: Sudden and intense flooding can catch people off guard.
  • Coastal Flooding: Caused by storm surges, high tides, or tsunamis.
  • Water Shortages: Affect drinking water supplies, agriculture, and industry.
  • Crop Failure: Can lead to food shortages and economic loss.
  • Wildfires: Dry conditions increase the risk of fires.
  • Property Damage: Can destroy homes, businesses, and infrastructure.
  • Air Quality: Smoke can cause respiratory issues and reduce visibility.
  • Loss of Vegetation: Can lead to soil erosion and habitat destruction.
  • Destruction of Infrastructure: Can bury roads, buildings, and utilities.
  • Loss of Life: Rapid and unpredictable movements can be deadly.
  • Blocked Rivers: Can create natural dams, leading to potential flooding upstream.
  • Heatwaves: Can cause heatstroke and dehydration, especially in vulnerable populations.
  • Wildfires: Increased risk due to dry conditions.
  • Strain on Power Grid: Increased demand for air conditioning can lead to power outages.
  • High Winds: Can cause extreme damage to buildings, vehicles, and trees.
  • Flying Debris: Can cause injuries or fatalities.
  • Inundation: Can flood coastal areas, destroying buildings and infrastructure.
  • Strong Currents: Can sweep people and debris out to sea.
