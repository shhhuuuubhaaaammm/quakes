Precautions

  • Emergency Kit: Keep an emergency supply kit with water, non-perishable food, medications, flashlight, batteries, first aid supplies, important documents, and other essentials.
  • Emergency Plan: Develop a family emergency plan that includes evacuation routes, meeting points, and communication strategies.
  • Communication: Have a list of emergency contacts and ensure all family members know how to contact each other.
  • Insurance: Ensure your home and property are adequately insured against natural disasters.
  • Stay Informed: Monitor weather forecasts and alerts through reliable sources such as radio, TV, or smartphone apps.
  • Education: Educate family members on emergency procedures and conduct regular drills.
  • Secure Objects: Anchor heavy furniture, appliances, and shelves to walls. Secure breakable items.
  • Safe Spots: Identify safe spots in each room, such as under sturdy tables or against interior walls.
  • Building Codes: Ensure your home complies with local seismic building codes.
  • During an Earthquake: Drop, Cover, and Hold On: Drop to your hands and knees, cover your head and neck, and hold on to sturdy furniture until the shaking stops.
  • Evacuation Plan: Know the evacuation routes and procedures for your area.
  • Stay Indoors: During an ashfall, stay indoors and keep windows and doors closed.
  • Protective Gear: Use masks and goggles to protect against ash inhalation and eye irritation.
  • Evacuation Plan: Know your community's hurricane evacuation routes and have a plan for where you will go.
  • Storm Shutters: Install storm shutters or board up windows and doors.
  • Secure Property: Bring in outdoor furniture, decorations, and anything else that could become a projectile.
  • Stockpile Supplies: Ensure you have enough food, water, and medications for several days.
  • Elevate Items: Elevate electrical appliances and utilities to higher floors or above potential flood levels.
  • Sandbags: Use sandbags to divert water away from your property.
  • Evacuation Plan: Know the safest route to higher ground and be prepared to evacuate if necessary.
  • Stay Away from Water: Avoid walking or driving through floodwaters.
  • Water Conservation: Implement water-saving measures such as fixing leaks, using drought-resistant plants, and reducing water usage.
  • Storage: Store water for emergency use.
  • Defensible Space: Create a defensible space around your home by clearing vegetation and debris.
  • Fire-Resistant Materials: Use fire-resistant building materials for roofs and exterior walls.
  • Evacuation Plan: Have an evacuation plan and know multiple routes out of your area.
  • Stay Informed: Monitor local fire conditions and follow evacuation orders immediately.
  • Stay Hydrated: Drink plenty of water and avoid strenuous activities during peak heat.
  • Cool Environment: Stay in air-conditioned buildings as much as possible.
  • Check on Vulnerable Individuals: Ensure the elderly, children, and those with health conditions are safe and cool.
  • Safe Room: Identify a safe room in your home, such as a basement or an interior room on the lowest floor without windows.
  • Emergency Kit: Keep a tornado emergency kit in your safe room.
  • Stay Informed: Monitor weather alerts and know the signs of an approaching tornado.
  • During a Tornado: Take cover immediately in your safe room or lie flat in a low-lying area if caught outdoors.
  • Evacuation Plan: Know the tsunami evacuation routes and high ground areas.
  • Stay Informed: Pay attention to tsunami warnings and alerts.
  • Immediate Action: If you feel an earthquake in a coastal area, move to higher ground immediately, as tsunamis can follow soon after.
